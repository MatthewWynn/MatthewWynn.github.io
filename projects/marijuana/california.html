California Marijuana Statistics

By Matthew Wynn and Kyle Russo

Since 2018, California has allocated nearly all of its yearly tax revenue from the commercial sale of marijuana to programs specifically targeted at the negative effects of the drug, particularly on young people’s physical and mental health and on the environment.

California’s style of revenue allocation — using money collected from legal marijuana sales to rectify wrongs incurred on historically less advantaged populations due to marijuana prohibition — may be becoming more popular, based on similar measures introduced in Maryland and enacted in Oregon.

Despite this, some California leaders are making a move to lower taxes on marijuana, therefore reducing the revenue that might be offered to these existing social programs, NBC News reported. Activists say this trend would disproportionately affect low-income children, mainly children who are non-white, by cutting the resources Proposition 64 funds.
