New Jersey Marijuana Statistics

By Matthew Wynn and Kyle Russo

New Jersey overwhelmingly voted to legalize recreational marijuana in November 2020, becoming the first Mid-Atlantic state to do so and achieving the largest margin of support ever for a marijuana referendum. Over two-thirds of voters supported it, with about 67 percent voting “yes.”

Taxes collected from the sale of marijuana will support social equity, according to the Marijuana Policy Project. 70 percent of the tax revenue is slated to assist what the state defines as “impact zones,” or areas that were disproportionately affected by drug enforcement.

New Jersey residents spent $3.5 million during the first three days of recreational dispensary sales, Forbes reported. Currently, 19 dispensaries in New Jersey sell both recreational and medicinal marijuana. Nine dispensaries sell strictly medicinal marijuana.
